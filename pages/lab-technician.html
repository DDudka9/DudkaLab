Position summary

The Dudka Lab seeks a motivated Research Technician / Lab Manager to help launch a new research group at Lehigh University. This is a foundational role: you will help build the lab from day one, set up shared protocols, run molecular biology and cell culture experiments, and train incoming students. The position is well-suited to a recent BS or MS graduate planning to apply to graduate school, medical school, or industry research within 1–3 years.

Day-to-day, you will combine experimental work (~70%) and lab operations (~30%): hands-on bench science alongside organization of reagents, equipment, ordering, and the mouse colony. You will work closely with Damian and the first lab members on flagship projects in chromosome biology and molecular evolution.

Responsibilities

Research (~70%)

  • Mammalian tissue culture, including mouse and human ESCs/iPSCs.
  • Standard molecular biology: cloning, PCR/qPCR, plasmid prep, Western blot.
  • CRISPR/Cas9 gene editing under supervision; genotyping of edited cell lines and mice.
  • Immunofluorescence staining, image acquisition on confocal/spinning disk microscopes, and basic image analysis (ImageJ/Fiji).
  • Mouse colony work: genotyping, breeding records, light husbandry support.
  • Contribute to manuscripts as a co-author when your work is part of the published study.

Lab management (~30%)

  • Maintain stocks of media, antibodies, primers, plasmids, and shared reagents.
  • Order consumables and equipment; track lab budget against project codes.
  • Maintain shared instruments (microscopes, centrifuges, biosafety cabinets) on a service schedule.
  • Onboard and train new students on standard lab protocols and safety.
  • Coordinate IACUC, IBC, and EHS compliance for the lab.

Qualifications

Required

  • BS or MS in biology, biochemistry, molecular biology, genetics, or a related field.
  • At least 6 months of hands-on bench experience (undergraduate research counts).
  • Comfort with sterile technique and tissue culture.
  • Strong organizational skills and attention to detail.
  • Ability to commit at least 12–24 months to the position.

Preferred

  • Prior experience with mammalian cell culture, mouse work, or fluorescence microscopy.
  • Familiarity with CRISPR/Cas9, cloning, or genotyping workflows.
  • Basic programming in Python or R (or willingness to learn — we'll train).
  • Experience as an undergraduate teaching assistant or peer tutor.

What we offer

  • Competitive salary (commensurate with experience) plus full Lehigh University benefits (health, dental, retirement, tuition remission).
  • A clear professional development plan: opportunities to attend at least one major conference per year, travel awards, and dedicated time for graduate / medical school applications during your final year.
  • Co-authorship on publications when your contribution merits it — discussed up front, not at submission.
  • Strong letters of recommendation. Damian's prior trainees have gone on to PhD programs at MIT, Cornell, and the University of Pennsylvania, and to MD-PhD programs at Jefferson.
  • Access to all of Lehigh's core facilities (microscopy, flow cytometry, animal research, genomics).
  • A culture of work-life balance: reasonable hours, no expectation of weekend or holiday work.

How to apply

Email dad526@lehigh.edu with the subject line "Technician application — Dudka Lab" and attach a single PDF that includes:

  • A short cover letter (1 page max) describing your research experience, your career goals over the next 1–5 years, and what excites you about this position.

About Lehigh University

Lehigh University is a private research university in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania, ~90 minutes from New York City and Philadelphia. The Department of Biological Sciences is housed in Iacocca Hall on the Mountaintop campus and offers core facilities for advanced microscopy, flow cytometry, and animal research. The Lehigh Valley is an affordable, family-friendly region with easy access to outdoor recreation and major cultural centers.

Lehigh University is an Equal Opportunity / Affirmative Action employer. Lehigh provides competitive benefits to qualifying domestic partners and is committed to a culturally and intellectually diverse academic community.
