Position summary

The Dudka Lab in the Department of Biological Sciences at Lehigh University is recruiting a postdoctoral researcher to study the rapid evolution of chromosome segregation. We use molecular evolution analyses, CRISPR gene editing, fluorescence microscopy, and mouse models to ask how the chromosome segregation machinery — proteins and DNA at the centromere — evolves so quickly despite an essential, conserved function, and what that means for fertility, development, and disease.

The successful candidate will lead an independent project aligned with one of the lab's three research directions, with substantial freedom to develop their own scientific questions. Candidates with strong experience in mammalian cell biology, mouse oocyte / embryo work, fluorescence microscopy, CRISPR/Cas9, or computational evolutionary analysis are especially encouraged to apply.

Research directions

  • Origin of rapid centromeric DNA evolution. Experimental evolution in mouse ESCs to test how DNA shape–mediated packaging shapes centromeric satellite expansion.
  • Functional impacts of adaptive protein evolution. CRISPR-engineered "mal-adapted" alleles in mouse to test how adaptive changes in centromeric proteins maintain faithful chromosome segregation and female fertility.
  • Health implications of mutations at adaptive sites. Human iPSCs to test how patient mutations at adaptive sites in centromeric proteins affect chromosome segregation and disease risk.

Qualifications

Required

  • PhD (or equivalent doctoral degree) in cell biology, molecular biology, genetics, computational biology, or a related field, awarded within the last three years (or expected within six months).
  • Demonstrated productivity (at least one first-author publication or preprint).
  • Strong experimental or computational skills directly relevant to one or more of the lab's research directions.
  • Excellent written and spoken English.
  • Ability to work collaboratively in a small, multi-disciplinary team.

Preferred

  • Experience with mouse oocytes, embryos, or pre-implantation development.
  • Quantitative fluorescence microscopy (live-cell, spinning disk, or super-resolution).
  • CRISPR/Cas9 gene editing in mammalian cells, including ESCs or iPSCs.
  • Comparative genomics, phylogenetics, or detection of positive selection.
  • Programming in Python or R for image analysis or sequence analysis.
  • Track record of fellowship applications (HFSP, EMBO, BIF, LSRF, HHWF, JCC, NSF PRFB).

What we offer

  • Two-year appointment with the possibility of renewal subject to performance and funding.
  • NIH NRSA-aligned salary plus full Lehigh University benefits (health, dental, retirement).
  • An annual professional development budget for conferences, courses, and travel.
  • Direct mentorship on every fellowship draft you submit (HFSP, EMBO, LSRF, HHWF, JCC, NSF PRFB, and others) — see the Join Us page for the full list of fellowships we will help you write.
  • Active collaborations with chromosome biology and molecular evolution labs in the US and Europe.
  • A new lab where you help shape the research direction and culture from day one.
