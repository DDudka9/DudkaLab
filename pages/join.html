External fellowships I'll help you write

Securing a fellowship sharpens the science, frees up the lab budget, and travels with you. I will work closely with you on drafts, feedback, and mock review. Below are the fellowships most relevant to chromosome biology, cell biology, and evolutionary genetics.

Postdoctoral · International
  • HFSP Long-Term / Cross-Disciplinary LOI initiation early May, LOI mid-May, full proposal late Sep
  • EMBO Postdoctoral Fellowship Two rounds per year: 4th Friday Jan, 2nd Friday Jul
  • Branco Weiss Fellowship Annual · Jan 15 deadline · any host worldwide
  • SNSF Postdoc.Mobility For Swiss-trained PhDs · two rounds per year
Postdoctoral · US
  • NIH F32 (Kirschstein-NRSA) Three rounds per year (Apr 8 · Aug 8 · Dec 8)
  • Life Sciences Research Foundation (LSRF) Annual · Oct 31 deadline · basic biology
  • Helen Hay Whitney Foundation Annual · mid-Jun deadline · basic biomedical
  • Jane Coffin Childs (JCC Fund) Annual · Feb 1 deadline · basic biomedical
Graduate / Predoctoral
  • NSF GRFP Annual · late Oct · US citizens / nationals / PRs
  • NIH F31 Predoctoral Fellowship Three rounds per year (Apr 8 · Aug 8 · Dec 8)
  • HHMI Gilliam Fellowship Annual · opens Sep 1 · check Lehigh institutional eligibility
  • Hertz Foundation Fellowship Annual · Oct 31 deadline · applied STEM focus

My commitment as a mentor

Joining a brand-new lab is exciting but it can also feel uncertain. Here's what I commit to — the same things I valued during my own training across.

1

Work-life balance

I encourage work efficiency not "long hours".

2

Weekly 1:1 meetings

We will talk science, troubleshoot, and plan your next steps.

3

Career planning

We will develop skills to help you reach your career goals.

4

Funding for development

I will promote you and your work at conferences and help you participate.

5

A real network

I will help you find the right collaborators and co-mentors.

6

Authorship transparency

If you contributed substantially, you're an author.

Get in touch

Interested in any of the above? Or just curious about the science? Email dad526@lehigh.edu with a short note about your background and what you find interesting in the lab — I read every email myself.
